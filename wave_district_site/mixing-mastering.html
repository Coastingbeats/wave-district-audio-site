About Your Engineer

Cameron Davis

Cameron’s approach to mixing is built around preserving impact instead of sanding off every rough edge. Rather than relying on endless editing, sample replacement, pitch correction (unless you want it or need it for your sound), or crushing everything for maximum loudness, the focus stays on dynamics, depth, natural ambience, and the relationships between instruments. Records should breathe — loud parts should actually feel louder because the quiet moments are allowed to exist.

Mastering is treated as the final step in presenting your record, not fixing it after the fact. Whether your music is a blown out hardcore EP, a dense metal record, an intimate singer-songwriter release, or something that doesn’t fit neatly into a genre, the goal is the same: make it translate everywhere while keeping the character, aggression, space, and humanity that made the performance worth recording in the first place.

No presets. No factory assembly line. Just records that sound honest, hit hard, and hold up for years instead of chasing whatever production trend happens to be popular this month.
